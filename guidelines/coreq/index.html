The COREQ guideline for writing a qualitative study.

About this guideline

This guideline is relevant to qualitative studies, especially those that use interviews, focus groups and surveys

Download Resources

Reporting guidelines are best used early:

  • Use a template to write quickly and avoid the blank page
  • Use a checklist to double-check and reassure others that your work is described transparently

Guidance

Approx. 1 min read

Research team and reflexivity

1. Personal Characteristics: Interviewer / facilitator

Which author / s conducted the interview or focus group?

2. Personal Characteristics: Credentials

What were the researcher’s credentials? E.g. PhD, MD

3. Personal Characteristics: Occupation

What was their occupation at the time of the study?

4. Personal Characteristics: Gender

Was the researcher male or female?

5. Personal Characteristics: Experience and training

What experience or training did the researcher have?

6. Relationship with participants Relationship established

Was a relationship established prior to study commencement?

Relationship with participants

7. Participant knowledge of the interviewer

What did the participants know about the researcher? e.g. personal goals, reasons for doing the research

8. Relationship with participants: Interviewer characteristics

What characteristics were reported about the interviewer / facilitator? e.g. Bias, assumptions, reasons and interests in the research topic

Study design

9. Theoretical framework: Methodological orientation and Theory

What methodological orientation was stated to underpin the study? e.g. grounded theory, discourse analysis, ethnography, phenomenology, content analysis

10. Participant selection: sampling

How were participants selected? e.g. purposive, convenience, consecutive, snowball

11. Participant selection: method of approach

How were participants approached? e.g. face-to-face, telephone, mail, email

12. Participant selection: sample size

How many participants were in the study?

13. Participant selection: non-participation

How many people refused to participate or dropped out? Reasons?

14. Setting: setting of data collection

Where was the data collected? e.g. home, clinic, workplace

15. Setting: prescence of non participants

Was anyone else present besides the participants and researchers?

16. Setting: description of sample

What are the important characteristics of the sample? e.g. demographic data, date

17. Data collection: interview guide

Were questions, prompts, guides provided by the authors? Was it pilot tested?

18. Data collection: repeat interviews

Were repeat interviews carried out? If yes, how many?

19. Data collection: audio / visual recording

Did the research use audio or visual recording to collect the data?

20. Data collection: field notes

Were field notes made during and / or after the interview or focus group?

21. Data collection: duration

What was the duration of the interviews or focus group?

22. Data collection: data saturation

Was data saturation discussed?

23. Data collection: transcripts returned

Were transcripts returned to participants for comment and / or correction?

Analysis and findings

24. Data analysis: number of data coders

How many data coders coded the data?

25. Data analysis: description of the coding tree

Did authors provide a description of the coding tree?

26. Data analysis: derivation of themes

Were themes identified in advance or derived from the data?

27. Data analysis: software

What software, if applicable, was used to manage the data?

28. Data analysis: participant checking

Did participants provide feedback on the findings?

29. Reporting: quotations presented

Were participant quotations presented to illustrate the themes / findings? Was each quotation identified? e.g. participant number

30. Reporting: data and findings consistent

Was there consistency between the data presented and the findings?

31. Reporting: clarity of major themes

Were major themes clearly presented in the findings?

32. Reporting: clarity of minor themes

Is there a description of diverse cases or discussion of minor themes?
